Here’s a **structured to‑do list** tailored for building your poetry book and website. It’s organized so you can move step‑by‑step without losing focus on the central point of your poems.

---

## 📖 Poetry Book To‑Do List
- **Define core theme** → Decide the central motif that ties your collection together.  
- **Group poems by theme** → Create sections (e.g., love, identity, nature, memory).  
- **Select anchor poems** → Choose 3–5 pieces that embody your voice and place them strategically.  
- **Format each poem** → Standardize title placement, stanza spacing, and typography.  
- **Write front matter** → Preface, dedication, acknowledgments.  
- **Add closing matter** → Author’s note or commentary.  
- **Proof and edit** → Ensure consistency in tone, spacing, and flow.  

---

## 🌐 Poetry Website To‑Do List
- **Design homepage** → Feature one rotating poem or showcase piece.  
- **Build archive** → Organize poems by tags or themes for easy browsing.  
- **Format poem pages** → Title at top, centered text, optional sidebar with metadata.  
- **Add engagement layer** → Audio readings, commentary, or newsletter signup.  
- **Create about page** → Share your creative journey and influences.  
- **Set navigation flow** → Next/Previous arrows or tag‑based browsing.  
- **Test responsiveness** → Ensure poems look good on mobile and desktop.  

---

## 🎯 Central Point Alignment
- **Voice consistency** → Keep tone and formatting unified across book and site.  
- **Reader journey** → Book = immersive, linear. Website = exploratory, interactive.  
- **Cross‑integration** → QR codes in book linking to website extras (audio, commentary).  

---
